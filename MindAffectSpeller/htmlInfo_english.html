MindAffect Speller

At MindAffect we build technology to control devices using brain activity: Brain Computer Interfaces, or BCIs. Using this app, an EEG headset and our Decoding device, you can communicate just by looking at the buttons that you wish to press.

Beneath you find some general information about MindAffect as well as help with using this app.

Background

The development of our BCI started at the Radboud University. Thanks to winning the Prize4Life, a project was started to bring this amazing technology to locked-in patients. The company MindAffect was founded, with the goal of helping patients, but also to apply BCI for other use cases.

We have demonstrated with a patient in Belgium that our BCI can help some locked-in patients. However, at this moment in time we have not reached the point of a certified, and hence sellable, product, nor have we found a business model to support such a solution in a sustainable way.

Therefore we decided to shift our focus from locked-in patients to developing a more general purpose development kit. This way we allow people around the world to assemble a BCI-solution, and tailor the application to their specific needs.

Are you interested in developing your own BCI? Please check out www.mindaffect.nl. We are also looking for companies that want to do a project together. Do you want to explore with us what the future of BCI will bring? Feel free to get in touch!

Help

This app is intended to be used together with an EEG headset and a MindAffect Decoder. Check out our site to complete your BCI setup: www.mindaffect.nl.

In order to control this app using your brain, follow these steps:

  1. Setup your MindAffect Decoder and EEG headset.
  2. Open the Settings app and connect your iPad to the Decoder's wifi network.
  3. Open this app, go to the Start screen and press the Brain button in the top-right corner.
  4. Follow the instructions in order to connect the app to the Decoder and calibrate the system.
  5. After exiting the Calibration screen, the buttons will begin to flicker. Now you should be able to press one by looking at it!

Beneath we clarify some things that might not immediately be clear.

Start screen

The Start screen provides some basic functions, such as saying predefined sentences or ringing a bell, as well as access to all other screens.

Connection indicator

If there is no connection with the MindAffect Decoder, a red dot will be visible in the bottom-right corner of the screen.

Start/stop flicker

By swiping up or down with one finger, you can start/stop the flickering.

Sleep screen

Because some users might not be able to swipe up or down, we added the Sleep screen. You enter this screen by pressing the Zzz button in the top-left of the Start screen. Enter this screen when you want a break, with minimal light and flickering.

Spelling screen

By pressing the keyboard icon in the Start screen, you enter the Spelling screen, where you can type text and let it be spoken out loud. Use the Pause button if you temporarily want to minimise the flickering.
